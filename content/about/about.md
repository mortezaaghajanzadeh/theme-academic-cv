## Morteza Aghajanzadeh

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Finance at the Stockholm School of Economics. My research lies at the intersection of entrepreneurial finance, innovation economics, and industrial organization. I combine theoretical modeling with empirical analysis to study how financial relationships shape market structure and the direction of innovative activity.

### Research Interests

**Venture Capital and Entrepreneurship.** I study how venture capital networks influence the formation, growth, and exit of new firms. My work examines how the structure of investor relationships affects the allocation of capital across industries and geographies, and how syndication patterns shape competition among startups.

**Innovation and Market Dynamics.** I am interested in how financial intermediaries — particularly venture capitalists and private equity investors — affect firms' incentives to innovate, enter new markets, and compete. I explore how investment decisions at the portfolio level influence aggregate market structure and the concentration of innovative activity.

**Theoretical Modeling and Empirical Methods.** My research combines contract theory and network models with empirical strategies based on large-scale administrative data. I use structural and reduced-form methods to identify causal effects of financing on firm behavior and market outcomes.
