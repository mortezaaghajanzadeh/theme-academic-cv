\begin{rSection}{Job Market Paper}
  \vspace{2pt}
  {\bf Two Faces of VC Networks: Performance Premium and Entry Deterrence} \\[2pt]
  {\small Venture capital firms co-invest through syndication networks, and a VC's position in that network is widely associated with stronger startup outcomes. This paper studies the two sides of that relationship. On one side, well-connected VCs enjoy a performance premium through better deal access and information sharing. On the other, the same network structure raises barriers that deter entry by new and peripheral investors, with consequences for competition in the market for startup capital. I assess the net efficiency implications for startup financing and the allocation of capital to entrepreneurial firms.}
\end{rSection}
